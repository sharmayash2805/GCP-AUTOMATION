Proposed Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI)

Green Credit Programme: Automation Engine

A Long-Term Verification & Allocation Platform for MoEFCC's 2023 Initiative

⚠️ Problem Statement

The Green Credit Programme (initiated late 2023) is currently in its infancy. Scaling it nationwide faces critical bottlenecks:

  • ✗ Manual Allocation: Fragmented state-level data makes finding degraded land difficult.
  • ✗ Opaque Verification: Reliance solely on physical officer visits is slow and prone to corruption.
  • ✗ Delayed Incentives: Credit issuance can take 12-24 months, discouraging corporate CSR participation.

🎯 Objectives

To build the foundational digital infrastructure required to scale the GCP over the next decade by:

  • ✓ Centralizing the national inventory of degraded lands using GIS.
  • ✓ AI-Assisted Verification using ISRO Satellite Imagery.
  • ✓ Creating a transparent, immutable ledger for Green Credit issuance and exchange.

🧮 Credit Calculation

The programmatic logic converting physical restoration into digital credits.

/* 1. Ecological Perf. Score */
EPS = (Trees_Planted × Survival_Rate)
/* 2. Final Credit Value */
Total_GC = EPS × Species_Factor × VCM
*Species_Factor: Ecological value multiplier per species
*VCM: Verification Confidence (AI + Admin Consensus)

🚀 Outcomes & Future Scope

Immediate Outcomes

  • ✔ Accelerated CSR: Connects corporate capital directly to degraded land.
  • ✔ AI-Assisted Trust: Reduces human verification effort by 90% while retaining final admin oversight.

Long-Term Vision (2026-2030)

Because the GCP is newly initiated, this architecture is built to scale drastically over the next decade:

🔗 Blockchain Integration
Moving credit management to a permissioned blockchain, treating credits as secure digital tokens.
🌍 Carbon Market Linking
Enabling Green Credits to be traded on international offset marketplaces.
🚁 Targeted Drone Audits
Deploying drones strictly for flagged cases requiring deeper manual verification, keeping core satellite costs low.
